## Basic Topic Description
<!-- Provide an overview of the project here. Explain the purpose and goals of the project. -->

The project challenge was titled **Human Powered Treasure**. The **Human Powered** aspect required us to generate power through human effort, while the **Treasure** aspect mandated the use of recycled materials.

Our goal was to use this challenge to develop a practical and versatile tool with real-world applications. We aimed not only to incorporate some recycled components but to use as many as possible. This approach helped minimize environmental impact and reduce costs.

## Idea and Inspiration
<!-- Describe the initial idea and the inspiration behind this project. Share any interesting insights or motivations. -->
During brainstorming, we explored multiple ideas but ultimately decided on a project called **Handyladen**, which translates to **Charge-Phone**. The inspiration for this idea came from a video by the YouTube channel [ElectroBOOM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wdgiTQffiwk), where the creator demonstrated how to charge a phone using the crank mechanism of a flashlight.

We further developed this idea by utilizing the power generated by a person while riding a bike. In addition to charging a phone, we aimed to extend the use of this power to a LED bike light with multiple lighting modes, such as a blinking mode and other settings.

However, we deliberately introduced a twist into our design: the cyclist can choose either to charge their phone or to power the bike light, but not both at the same time. This forces the user to decide between staying connected to their social life or prioritizing safety on the road.
